## Equal Employment Opportunity

[CLIENT NAME] is an equal opportunity employer. We do not discriminate based on race, color, religion, sex, national origin, age, disability, genetic information, veteran status, or any other characteristic protected by federal, state, or local law.

North Carolina does not have a comprehensive state civil rights act covering private employers, so federal standards (Title VII, ADA, ADEA) apply. Employers with 15+ employees are covered by Title VII and ADA; employers with 20+ employees are covered by ADEA.

## At-Will Employment

North Carolina is an at-will employment state. This means:

- Employment may be terminated by either the employer or employee at any time
- No cause or advance notice is required by either party
- This handbook does not create a contract or guarantee of employment
- Only the [OWNER/PRESIDENT] of [CLIENT NAME] has authority to modify at-will status, and only in writing

## Work Hours and Attendance

### Standard Work Week

The standard work week is Monday through Friday. Regular business hours are [8:00 AM to 5:00 PM / CLIENT-SPECIFIC]. Actual schedules may vary by department and role.

### Overtime

Non-exempt employees are eligible for overtime pay at 1.5× their regular rate for hours worked over 40 in a workweek. North Carolina follows the federal FLSA overtime threshold. Overtime must be approved by a supervisor in advance.

**Note:** North Carolina does not require daily overtime — only weekly overtime applies.

### Attendance

### Family and Medical Leave (FMLA)

Employees who have worked for [CLIENT NAME] for at least 12 months and at least 1,250 hours are eligible for up to 12 weeks of unpaid, job-protected leave under the FMLA. Qualifying reasons include:

- Serious health condition of the employee
- Serious health condition of an immediate family member
- Qualifying military exigency

FMLA applies to employers with 50 or more employees within a 75-mile radius.

## Workplace Safety

[CLIENT NAME] is committed to providing a safe working environment. All employees are responsible for:

- Following safety rules and using required protective equipment
- Reporting unsafe conditions to their supervisor immediately
- Reporting all injuries, no matter how minor, within 24 hours

### Workers' Compensation

North Carolina requires employers to carry workers' compensation insurance for businesses with 3 or more employees. If you are injured on the job, report the injury to your supervisor immediately. Workers' comp covers medical expenses and partial wage replacement for work-related injuries.

## Separation of Employment

### Voluntary Resignation

Employees who wish to resign are asked to provide at least two weeks' written notice. This is a request, not a requirement — North Carolina is an at-will state.

### Involuntary Termination

[CLIENT NAME] reserves the right to terminate employment at any time for any lawful reason. Terminated employees will receive their final paycheck by the next regular payday.

**North Carolina Final Pay:** Employers must pay all wages due by the next regular payday after termination. There is no requirement for immediate payment.

### Return of Property

All company property (keys, equipment, documents, ID badges) must be returned on or before the last day of employment.
