## Purpose

This document provides a single reference for all leave types available to employees. It summarizes eligibility, duration, and key requirements for each leave category. For full policy details, refer to the standalone policy documents referenced in each section.

## Paid Time Off (PTO)

### Accrual-Based PTO (Standard)

Applies to most employees. PTO accrues per pay period based on tenure:

| Tenure | Annual PTO |
|--------|-----------|
| 0–1 years | Per company policy |
| 1–5 years | Per company policy |
| 5+ years | Per company policy |

- Unused PTO rollover and payout at termination are governed by company policy and state law
- North Carolina treats earned PTO as wages that must be paid out at separation if the employer's policy provides for accrual
- Request PTO through the company's time-off system

### Flexible (Unlimited) PTO

Available to full-time exempt (salaried) employees at tech/startup clients after completing the 90-day introductory period.

- Part-time, non-exempt (hourly), temporary, and contract employees are not eligible
- "Unlimited" does not mean "unrestricted" — requests require advance notice and manager approval
- Minimum recommended: 15 days per year (excluding company holidays)
- No payout on separation since PTO does not accrue
- See the Flexible PTO Policy for full guidelines on request windows, blackout periods, and abuse patterns

## Family and Medical Leave (FMLA)

**Eligibility:** Employees who have worked 12+ months and 1,250+ hours at a location with 50+ employees within 75 miles.

**Entitlement:** Up to 12 workweeks of unpaid, job-protected leave per 12-month period for:

- Birth or placement of a child (within one year)
- Serious health condition of the employee
- Serious health condition of a spouse, child, or parent
- Qualifying military exigency

**Military caregiver leave:** Up to 26 workweeks in a single 12-month period to care for a covered service member with a serious injury or illness.

**Key requirements:**
- 30 days' advance notice for foreseeable leave
- Medical certification may be required
- Health insurance continues during leave on the same terms
- Employee is entitled to restoration to the same or equivalent position

See the FMLA Policy for full certification, notice, and coordination details.

## Sick Leave

North Carolina does not mandate paid sick leave for private employers. Company-provided sick leave is a courtesy benefit with days per year set by company policy.

- Sick leave may run concurrently with FMLA leave if the absence qualifies
- Under the Flexible PTO Policy, sick days (self or family member care) are covered under the general PTO allowance

## Workers' Compensation Leave

Employees injured on the job are covered by workers' compensation insurance, which provides:

- Medical expense coverage for work-related injuries
- Partial wage replacement during recovery
- Job protection during the recovery period (per state law)

**Reporting requirement:** Report all workplace injuries within 24 hours, no matter how minor.

Workers' compensation leave runs concurrently with FMLA leave when the injury also qualifies as a serious health condition. North Carolina requires coverage for businesses with 3+ employees.

See the Workers' Compensation State Reference for state-specific thresholds, rates, and exemptions.

## Short-Term Disability

Short-term disability provides partial wage replacement for non-work-related injuries or illnesses that prevent an employee from performing their job duties.

- Runs concurrently with FMLA leave when the condition qualifies
- Specific benefits (waiting period, duration, percentage of pay) are set by the company's disability insurance plan
- Coordinate with HR and the benefits administrator for filing procedures

## Bereavement Leave

**Note:** Bereavement leave details are set by individual company policy. Common provisions include:

- 3–5 days of paid leave for the death of an immediate family member (spouse, child, parent, sibling)
- 1–3 days for extended family members (grandparent, in-law, aunt, uncle)
- Additional unpaid time may be available through PTO or FMLA (if the situation involves a serious health condition)

*Confirm your company's specific bereavement policy with HR.*

## Jury Duty Leave

Employees summoned for jury duty are entitled to time off to serve.

- North Carolina prohibits employers from discharging or demoting employees for responding to a jury summons
- Whether jury duty leave is paid or unpaid depends on company policy
- Employees should notify their supervisor as soon as they receive a summons and provide documentation

---

## Voting Leave

- North Carolina does not have a specific voting leave statute, but employers are encouraged to allow reasonable time for employees to vote
- Several other states in Truepoint's service area (e.g., Tennessee, Maryland) require paid voting leave — consult state-specific requirements

---

## Military Service Leave (USERRA)

The Uniformed Services Employment and Reemployment Rights Act (USERRA) provides:

- Up to 5 cumulative years of leave for military service
- Reemployment rights upon return from service
- Continuation of health insurance for up to 24 months during military leave
- Protection from discrimination based on military service or obligation

This is a federal right — no company policy opt-in is required. USERRA applies to all employers regardless of size.

## Leave Coordination Summary

Multiple leave types may overlap. When they do, they generally run concurrently (not sequentially):

| Situation | Leave Types That May Overlap |
|-----------|------------------------------|
| Serious personal illness | FMLA + Sick Leave + Short-Term Disability |
| Work-related injury | Workers' Comp + FMLA (if qualifying) |
| Birth of a child | FMLA + PTO (if company allows concurrent use) |
| Military deployment | USERRA + FMLA (for qualifying exigency) |
