# Family and Medical Leave Act (FMLA) Policy

**Prepared by:** Truepoint HR Solutions
**Applicable:** Employers with 50+ employees within 75-mile radius
**Last Updated:** 2023
**Status:** Active — Current FMLA policy for covered employers

---

## Eligibility

To qualify for FMLA leave, an employee must:

1. Have worked for [CLIENT NAME] for at least **12 months** (need not be consecutive)
2. Have worked at least **1,250 hours** during the 12 months before leave begins
3. Work at a location where the employer has **50 or more employees** within a 75-mile radius

---

## Leave Entitlement

Eligible employees may take up to **12 workweeks** of unpaid, job-protected leave in a 12-month period for:

- **Birth of a child** and care of the newborn within one year of birth
- **Placement of a child** for adoption or foster care within one year of placement
- **Serious health condition** of the employee that makes them unable to perform essential job functions
- **Serious health condition** of the employee's spouse, child, or parent
- **Qualifying exigency** arising from a family member's active duty or call to active duty in the Armed Forces

### Military Caregiver Leave

Eligible employees may take up to **26 workweeks** in a single 12-month period to care for a covered service member with a serious injury or illness (if the employee is the spouse, child, parent, or next of kin).

---

## Serious Health Condition

A "serious health condition" means an illness, injury, impairment, or physical or mental condition involving:

- Inpatient care (overnight stay in a hospital, hospice, or residential medical facility)
- Continuing treatment by a health care provider, including:
  - Incapacity for more than 3 consecutive calendar days with continuing treatment
  - Pregnancy or prenatal care
  - Chronic conditions requiring periodic treatment
  - Permanent or long-term conditions
  - Conditions requiring multiple treatments (e.g., chemotherapy, dialysis)

---

## How to Request Leave

### Employee Responsibilities

- **Foreseeable leave:** Provide at least **30 days' advance notice** when the need for leave is foreseeable (e.g., expected birth, scheduled surgery)
- **Unforeseeable leave:** Notify [CLIENT NAME] as soon as practicable, generally the same day or next business day
- Provide sufficient information to determine whether the leave qualifies under FMLA
- Comply with normal call-in procedures when able

### Employer Responsibilities

- Notify the employee within **5 business days** whether the leave qualifies as FMLA
- Provide written notice of rights and responsibilities
- Designate leave as FMLA-qualifying and notify the employee in writing

---

## Certification

[CLIENT NAME] may require a medical certification from a health care provider to support a request for FMLA leave. The certification must include:

- Date the condition began
- Probable duration
- Appropriate medical facts
- Statement that the employee is unable to perform job functions (for employee's own condition)
- Statement that the employee is needed to care for the family member (for family member's condition)

[CLIENT NAME] may request recertification every 30 days if circumstances change.

---

## Intermittent and Reduced Schedule Leave

FMLA leave may be taken intermittently or on a reduced schedule when medically necessary. Examples:

- Recurring medical appointments
- Flare-ups of a chronic condition
- Reduced work hours during recovery

[CLIENT NAME] may temporarily transfer the employee to an equivalent position that better accommodates intermittent leave.

---

## Job Protection

On return from FMLA leave, employees are entitled to:

- Restoration to the **same position** or an **equivalent position** with equivalent pay, benefits, and conditions
- Continuation of group health insurance during leave on the same terms as if the employee had continued to work

### Key Employee Exception

[CLIENT NAME] may deny restoration to a "key employee" (salaried, among highest-paid 10%) if restoration would cause substantial and grievous economic injury. The employee must be notified of key employee status when leave is requested.

---

## Benefits During Leave

- **Health insurance:** [CLIENT NAME] will maintain group health insurance coverage during FMLA leave. The employee must continue to pay their share of premiums.
- **Other benefits:** Benefits that accrue based on hours worked (e.g., PTO) do not accrue during unpaid FMLA leave unless company policy provides otherwise.

---

## Coordination with Other Leave

FMLA leave runs concurrently with:

- Paid sick leave (if applicable)
- Short-term disability
- Workers' compensation leave (if the injury qualifies as a serious health condition)

Employees may elect — or [CLIENT NAME] may require — substitution of accrued paid leave for unpaid FMLA leave.
