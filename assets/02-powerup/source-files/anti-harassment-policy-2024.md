# Anti-Harassment Policy — 2024 Update

**Prepared by:** Truepoint HR Solutions
**Applicable States:** All (federal baseline with state-specific notes)
**Last Updated:** March 2024
**Status:** Active — Aligns with EEOC Guidance on Harassment in the Workplace (2024)

---

## Purpose

This policy prohibits all forms of harassment in the workplace and establishes procedures for reporting, investigating, and resolving complaints. This update incorporates the EEOC's 2024 guidance on harassment prevention.

---

## Scope

This policy applies to all employees, applicants, contractors, vendors, clients, and visitors. It covers conduct occurring:

- On company premises
- At company-sponsored events
- During business travel
- Through electronic communications (email, chat, social media, video calls)
- In any context where conduct could affect the work environment

---

## Prohibited Conduct

### Sexual Harassment

Unwelcome sexual advances, requests for sexual favors, and other verbal or physical conduct of a sexual nature when:

- Submission is made a condition of employment (quid pro quo)
- Submission or rejection affects employment decisions
- The conduct unreasonably interferes with work or creates a hostile environment

### Other Harassment

Harassment based on race, color, religion, sex (including pregnancy, sexual orientation, and gender identity), national origin, age (40+), disability, genetic information, or veteran status. Examples include:

- Slurs, epithets, or derogatory comments
- Offensive jokes, images, or objects
- Threats, intimidation, or bullying
- Interference with work performance

### 2024 EEOC Guidance — Key Updates

The EEOC's 2024 enforcement guidance clarifies:

- **Virtual harassment** is actionable — conduct via video calls, chat, or social media can create a hostile environment
- **Single severe incidents** can constitute harassment without requiring a pattern
- **Sex-based harassment** includes conduct based on sexual orientation and gender identity
- **Bystander reporting** is protected — employees who report harassment they witness (not just experience) are protected from retaliation

## Reporting Procedures

### How to Report

Employees who experience or witness harassment should report it through any of these channels:

1. **Direct supervisor** (unless the supervisor is involved)
2. **HR department** — [HR CONTACT NAME], [EMAIL], [PHONE]
3. **Anonymous hotline** — [NUMBER]
4. **Written complaint** — Submit to HR in writing

### No Retaliation

[CLIENT NAME] strictly prohibits retaliation against anyone who reports harassment, participates in an investigation, or opposes harassment. Retaliation includes adverse employment actions, exclusion, or any other negative treatment.

### Obligation to Report

Supervisors and managers who receive a harassment complaint or observe harassment are required to report it to HR within 24 hours, regardless of whether the affected employee requests action.

---

## Investigation Process

1. **Acknowledge** — HR acknowledges receipt within 24 hours
2. **Assess** — Determine scope and assign investigator (internal HR or external, depending on severity)
3. **Investigate** — Interview complainant, respondent, and witnesses. Review relevant documents and communications.
4. **Determine** — Make findings based on preponderance of evidence
5. **Act** — Implement appropriate corrective action
6. **Follow up** — Check in with complainant to ensure no retaliation

Investigations are conducted as confidentially as possible, consistent with a thorough investigation.

---

## Corrective Action

Employees found to have engaged in harassment will face disciplinary action proportional to the severity of the offense. Actions may include:

- Verbal or written warning
- Mandatory training
- Suspension without pay
- Demotion or reassignment
- Termination

---

## Training

All employees receive anti-harassment training:

- **New hires:** During onboarding (within first 30 days)
- **All employees:** Annual refresher training
- **Supervisors and managers:** Additional training on reporting obligations and investigation procedures
