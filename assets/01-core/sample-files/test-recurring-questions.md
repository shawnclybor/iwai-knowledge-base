# Test — Top Recurring Internal Questions

*Compiled from stakeholder interviews with Lisa Chen (Principal), Marcus Williams (Senior Consultant), and Priya Sharma (Consultant). These represent the questions asked most frequently via Slack and direct message to Lisa Chen and Marcus Williams.*

---

## Template Selection & Version Control

1. **Which employee handbook template should I use for [client type + state]?** — Asked by all consultants, multiple times per week. The answer depends on client industry (restaurant, construction, medical, general) and state. No reliable signal in Drive for which file is current. Priya used the wrong NC version for three weeks before anyone caught it.

2. **Is this the most current version of [template]?** — Asked constantly when pulling files from Drive. Version labels ("MASTER," "v2," "USE THIS ONE") are inconsistent and not reliable. Consultants cross-reference with each other or ask Marcus/Lisa to confirm.

3. **Where is the [state]-specific version of [template]?** — NC, SC, VA, and GA versions exist for some templates but not others. Consultants often can't tell whether a state-specific version exists or if they should adapt a general template themselves.

4. **What's the minimum wage for [state] right now?** — Asked when building or updating handbooks, especially for restaurant and construction clients. Marcus maintains a quarterly-updated tracking spreadsheet but most consultants don't know it exists. The Georgia state rate ($5.15) vs. federal floor ($7.25) has already caused at least one documented error.

---

## Compliance Questions — Client-Facing

5. **Can we fire this employee for [reason]?** — Asked weekly, usually framed around "bad attitude," performance, or conduct. Answer depends on state at-will doctrine, whether documentation exists, and whether the client has a handbook with progressive discipline. Lisa and Marcus recited a near-identical response word-for-word, confirming this is highly predictable.

6. **Does [policy] apply to my client? They have [X] employees.** — Threshold-based rules (FMLA at 50, ADA at 15, COBRA) generate constant questions. The FMLA Policy - under 50 template exists in Drive and is legally incorrect — consultants may be handing clients a policy that doesn't apply to them.

7. **What does my client need to do for COBRA when an employee leaves?** — Asked weekly. Answer varies by client size. No single reference document.

8. **Is this person exempt or non-exempt?** — Classification questions come up regularly for clients adding new roles or changing existing ones. The answer depends on salary level, salary basis test, and duties test. No classification reference guide exists.

9. **What harassment training does my client need to do?** — State requirements vary significantly (California, New York have specific mandates; NC does not). The 2024 anti-harassment policy update doesn't include a training requirements reference.

10. **My client is in [state] and wants to [policy change]. What do they need to know?** — Multi-state compliance questions for clients with operations in more than one state. Restaurant and construction clients are the most common cases. No multi-state reference guide exists.

---

## Construction-Specific Compliance

11. **Does my client need to pay prevailing wage on this project?** — Applies to government contracts (Davis-Bacon). Answer depends on project type, contract value, and funding source. Knowledge lives entirely in Marcus's head.

12. **How do I handle workers' comp for a crew working in multiple states?** — Construction clients frequently move crews across NC, SC, and VA. Requirements vary by state. Marcus's Workers Comp State-by-State Reference.xlsx is the only reliable source, and most consultants don't know it exists.

13. **What are the OSHA requirements for [construction activity]?** — Construction clients ask regularly about specific OSHA standards. Marcus handles all of these; no documentation of common answers.

---

## Restaurant-Specific Compliance

14. **Can my client take a tip credit for [employee type]?** — Tipped minimum wage and tip credit rules vary by state and role. NC and SC have different rules. No consolidated tip credit reference exists.

15. **How do we set up a tip pool legally?** — DOL tip pooling rules changed in 2018 and 2020. Restaurant clients ask this frequently. The restaurant handbook template has tip pooling language but it was last verified February 2024.

---

## Cross-Coverage & Client Handoffs

16. **What do I need to know about [colleague]'s client before I cover for them?** — No handoff document format exists. Covering consultants reconstruct context from email threads. Two client losses are attributed to poor coverage continuity.

17. **Which clients are mid-PEO transition right now?** — PEO transitions require knowing which systems have migrated and which haven't. Priya's NovaByte client has three employees still on PEO for benefits. No master list or brief format exists.

---

## Internal Process & Escalation

18. **Do I need Lisa to review this before I send it to the client?** — No escalation framework exists. Priya sends everything. Marcus sends selectively. Lisa had 47 items in her review queue in a single week. The answer should depend on document type, risk level, and consultant experience — but no guidance exists.

19. **Who handles [topic] when [colleague] is out?** — No routing guide or skills directory. Consultants default to Slacking Lisa regardless of topic.

20. **How does Truepoint handle [client type] differently from [other client type]?** — New consultants don't know the firm's segmented approach — restaurants vs. construction vs. medical vs. tech have meaningfully different scopes and compliance postures. No orientation document explains this.
