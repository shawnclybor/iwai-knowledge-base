## Pain Points Identified

1. **Lisa is the single point of failure for institutional knowledge** — Receives 12–15 Slack messages or texts per day from consultants asking operational and compliance questions. No documented fallback when she's unavailable.
   - Raised by: Lisa Chen, corroborated by Marcus Williams and Priya Sharma
   - Quote: "I am the bottleneck. Like, I don't want to be, but I am."

2. **Template chaos — hundreds of files, no reliable way to find the current version** — Drive contains 400–500 documents (templates, state variations, duplicates, old versions). Estimated 150 unique current templates, but identifying them requires institutional memory.
   - Raised by: Lisa Chen, Marcus Williams, Priya Sharma
   - Quote: "Search the drive and pray." — Marcus Williams

3. **Compliance risk from incorrect templates** — Priya used an outdated NC employee handbook template for her first three weeks — wrong minimum wage, wrong version — and nobody caught it until Lisa reviewed by chance.
   - Raised by: Lisa Chen, Priya Sharma

4. **New consultant ramp time is 5–6 months** — Priya reported ~8–10 real mistakes in her first months, all tied to undocumented institutional knowledge. No onboarding guide exists.
   - Raised by: Priya Sharma

5. **Cross-coverage gaps** — When covering a colleague's clients, consultants have no access to client context. They either scramble through email or call Lisa. Two clients have been lost due to poor coverage continuity.
   - Raised by: Marcus Williams, Priya Sharma, Lisa Chen

6. **Repetitive client Q&A consuming billable time** — Consultants answer the same compliance questions weekly (termination, COBRA, exempt/non-exempt). Answers are context-dependent by state, industry, and company size, so each response requires synthesizing from multiple sources.
   - Raised by: Marcus Williams, Lisa Chen

7. **No routing guide for internal questions** — Consultants don't know who to ask or where to look for different question types. No directory or escalation path exists.
   - Raised by: Priya Sharma

## Current Workflows Described

1. **Finding a template** — Search Drive, open several results, compare to identify the most current version, ask Lisa if unsure. No reliable signal for which file is canonical.
   - Described by: Marcus Williams, Lisa Chen

2. **Answering a client compliance question** — Research in Drive (30–40 minutes), Slack Lisa, or wait. If Lisa is in a meeting, consultant either waits or guesses.
   - Described by: Marcus Williams, Lisa Chen

3. **Cross-coverage** — When covering a colleague, the covering consultant reads email threads to reconstruct client context. No handoff documentation exists.
   - Described by: Marcus Williams, Priya Sharma

4. **Client onboarding knowledge** — Learned through trial and error and correction by Lisa. No structured onboarding guide or process document.
   - Described by: Priya Sharma
