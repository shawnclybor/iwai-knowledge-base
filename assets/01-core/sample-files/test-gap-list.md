# Test — Knowledge Gap List

*Compiled from discovery interviews, content audit, and storage inventory review. Each gap represents a question asked regularly by staff that has no reliable documented answer.*

---

## How to Read This List

Each gap includes:

- **Question** — How the question is typically asked
- **Who asks** — Role(s) and frequency
- **Current workaround** — How it's handled today
- **Why it's a gap** — What's missing and what risk it creates
- **Recommended KB file** — Where the answer should live once the knowledge base is built

---

## Gap 1: Template Index — Which Version Is Current?

**Question:** "Which employee handbook template should I use for [client type + state]? Is this the most current version?"
**Who asks:** All consultants; multiple times per week
**Current workaround:** Ask Marcus or Lisa to confirm. Search Drive and compare dates, hoping version labels are accurate. They are not.
**Why it's a gap:** Template storage is fragmented across 5 folders. Version labels (MASTER, v2, USE THIS ONE, CURRENT) are inconsistent and unreliable. The NC handbook in Client Templates has the wrong minimum wage and was used by Priya for 3 weeks before anyone caught it. No master index maps document types to their canonical version.
**Recommended KB file:** `template-index.md`

---

## Gap 2: State Minimum Wage — Current Rates and Traps

**Question:** "What's the minimum wage for [state] right now?"
**Who asks:** All consultants; whenever building or updating a handbook or offer letter
**Current workaround:** Search Drive (unreliable — State Law Summaries are 2021–2022), ask Marcus, or search the web independently.
**Why it's a gap:** Marcus maintains a quarterly-updated tracking spreadsheet (Workers Comp State-by-State Reference.xlsx) that most consultants don't know exists. Georgia's state minimum wage ($5.15) is below the federal floor ($7.25) — a trap that has already caused a documented error. No single KB reference exists that combines current rates with contextual notes about state-specific traps.
**Recommended KB file:** `state-compliance-reference.md`

## Gap 3: Employment Threshold Rules — When Does [Law] Apply?

**Question:** "Does [policy] apply to my client? They have [X] employees."
**Who asks:** All consultants; weekly
**Current workaround:** Ask Lisa. The FMLA Policy - under 50 template exists in Drive and is legally incorrect — consultants may be using it.
**Why it's a gap:** Threshold-based laws (FMLA at 50, ADA at 15, COBRA, Title VII at 15) generate constant questions. No reference document maps each law to its employee count threshold, employer type, and scope. The FMLA - under 50 template is an active liability.
**Recommended KB file:** `state-compliance-reference.md`

## Gap 10: Restaurant — Tip Credit and Tipped Minimum Wage

**Question:** "Can my client take a tip credit for [employee type]? How do we set up a tip pool legally?"
**Who asks:** Consultants with restaurant clients; frequently
**Current workaround:** Ask Lisa or Marcus. The restaurant handbook template has tip pooling language but currency was last verified February 2024.

## Gap 11: Cross-Coverage — Client Briefs

**Question:** "What do I need to know about [colleague]'s client before I cover for them?"
**Who asks:** All consultants; when covering for a colleague on vacation or leave

**Why it's a gap:** No client brief format exists. Complex clients (multi-state operations, mid-PEO transition, restaurant with tip credit, construction with government contracts) require more than a hallway conversation to hand off safely. Lisa cited client loss as a direct consequence.

## Gap 12: Escalation Framework

**Question:** "Do I need Lisa to review this before I send it to the client?"
**Who asks:** All consultants; constantly — Priya escalates everything, Marcus escalates selectively, neither has a framework

**Why it's a gap:** No defined escalation tiers exist. Lisa described her own rules as inconsistent and "in my head." The result is over-escalation, a 47-item review queue, and bottlenecked delivery. A collaborative session to build the framework is scheduled but has not yet occurred.

## Gap 13: Truepoint Client Type Orientation

**Question:** "How does Truepoint handle [client type] differently from [other client type]?"
**Who asks:** New consultants; during onboarding and first months on the job
**Current workaround:** Trial and error. Lisa corrects mistakes. Priya's 5–6 month ramp time is largely attributed to the absence of this orientation.

**Recommended KB file:** `truepoint-orientation.md`
