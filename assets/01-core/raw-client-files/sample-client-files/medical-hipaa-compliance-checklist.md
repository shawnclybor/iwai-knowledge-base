## Purpose

This checklist is designed to help [CLIENT NAME] assess and maintain compliance with the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA) as it relates to the practice's role as both a **Covered Entity** (healthcare provider) and an **Employer** handling employee health information.

**Important Distinction:** HIPAA's Privacy Rule applies to [CLIENT NAME] in two contexts:
1. **As a healthcare provider** — protecting patient PHI (managed by clinical compliance, outside Truepoint's scope)
2. **As an employer** — protecting employee health information in HR records, benefits administration, and workers' comp

This checklist addresses **Context 2 only** — the employer-side HIPAA obligations.

## Section 2: Employee Health Information — HR Records

- [ ] Employee medical files are stored **separately** from general personnel files
- [ ] Medical files are in a locked cabinet or password-protected digital folder
- [ ] Access to medical files is limited to: [LIST AUTHORIZED PERSONNEL]
- [ ] FMLA documentation stored in medical file, not personnel file
- [ ] ADA accommodation requests and documentation stored in medical file
- [ ] Workers' compensation records stored separately from personnel file
- [ ] Drug test results stored in medical file with restricted access
- [ ] Pre-employment physical results (if any) stored in medical file

### Common Violations Found in Previous Audits

| Issue | Risk Level | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Medical notes filed in general personnel folder | HIGH | Found in 3 of 4 location audits (2023) |
| FMLA paperwork accessible to office managers | MEDIUM | Office managers do not need access to medical details |
| Drug test results stored in unlocked desk drawer | HIGH | Found at [LOCATION REDACTED] |
| No BAA with IT company managing cloud storage | MEDIUM | PHI potentially accessible without proper safeguards |

## Section 3: Benefits Administration

- [ ] Health plan enrollment forms contain PHI — stored securely
- [ ] Employees' benefit elections not visible to unauthorized staff
- [ ] Open enrollment communications do not disclose individual health information
- [ ] Benefits summary documents do not include individual employee data
- [ ] COBRA notices handled in compliance with privacy requirements
- [ ] Health plan Summary Plan Description (SPD) provided to eligible employees

## Section 5: Breach Response

- [ ] Written breach notification procedure in place
- [ ] Breach risk assessment process documented
- [ ] Staff know who to contact if a potential breach is discovered
- [ ] Breach log maintained (even if no breaches have occurred — document that)

### Breach Notification Requirements (Reminder)

| Notification To | Threshold | Deadline |
|---|---|---|
| Affected individuals | Any unsecured PHI breach | 60 days from discovery |
| HHS (via online portal) | Breach affecting 500+ individuals | 60 days from discovery |
| HHS (annual log) | Breach affecting fewer than 500 individuals | Within 60 days of end of calendar year |
| Local media | Breach affecting 500+ individuals in one state/jurisdiction | 60 days from discovery |

---

## Section 6: Physical & Technical Safeguards (HR-Relevant)

- [ ] HR workstations with PHI access have password-protected screensavers
- [ ] PHI is not visible on screens in common areas
- [ ] Printed documents containing PHI are shredded, not recycled
- [ ] Email containing PHI is encrypted or sent through secure portal
- [ ] Remote access to HR systems requires multi-factor authentication
- [ ] Former employees' access to systems terminated promptly upon separation
