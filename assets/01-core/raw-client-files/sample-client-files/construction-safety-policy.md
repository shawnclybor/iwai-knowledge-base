## Purpose

This policy establishes the safety standards and procedures for all [CLIENT NAME] employees, subcontractors, and site visitors. Compliance with this policy and all applicable OSHA regulations is a condition of employment.

---

## Scope

This policy applies to:
- All full-time and part-time employees of [CLIENT NAME]
- Temporary and seasonal workers assigned to [CLIENT NAME] job sites
- Subcontractors working on [CLIENT NAME]-managed projects
- Visitors to any [CLIENT NAME] job site

**Multi-State Note:** Where state safety requirements differ from federal OSHA standards, the more stringent standard applies. North Carolina and South Carolina operate under federal OSHA jurisdiction. Virginia operates under its own State Plan (VOSH) which includes additional requirements noted throughout this document.

## Responsibilities

### Management
- Provide a safe and healthful workplace for all employees
- Ensure all employees receive required safety training before beginning work
- Maintain required safety equipment and personal protective equipment (PPE)
- Conduct regular job site safety inspections
- Investigate all incidents and near-misses within 24 hours

### Employees
- Follow all safety rules and procedures
- Use required PPE at all times on job sites
- Report unsafe conditions immediately to their supervisor
- Report all injuries, no matter how minor, within 24 hours
- Participate in all required safety training

### Subcontractors
- Comply with all [CLIENT NAME] safety policies in addition to their own
- Provide documentation of safety training for their workers
- Carry minimum required insurance coverage: [AMOUNTS REDACTED]

## Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)

The following PPE is required on all job sites unless a site-specific safety plan indicates otherwise:

| PPE Item | Required For | Standard |
|---|---|---|
| Hard hat | All personnel on active job sites | ANSI Z89.1 |
| Safety glasses | All personnel on active job sites | ANSI Z87.1 |
| High-visibility vest | All personnel on active job sites | ANSI/ISEA 107 Class 2 minimum |
| Steel-toe boots | All personnel on active job sites | ASTM F2413 |
| Hearing protection | Exposure above 85 dB TWA | ANSI S3.19 |
| Fall protection harness | Work at heights above 6 feet | ANSI Z359.11 |
| Respiratory protection | As specified by site hazard assessment | NIOSH approved |

**[CLIENT NAME] provides:** Hard hats, safety glasses, high-visibility vests, hearing protection, and fall protection equipment. Employees are responsible for providing their own steel-toe boots.

## Fall Protection

Falls are the leading cause of death in the construction industry. [CLIENT NAME] requires:

- **100% fall protection** for work at heights of 6 feet or more (OSHA 1926.501)
- Guardrail systems, safety net systems, or personal fall arrest systems must be in place before work begins
- All fall protection equipment must be inspected before each use
- Damaged equipment must be removed from service immediately

**Virginia-Specific:** VOSH requires a written fall protection plan for all work at heights above 6 feet, even when conventional fall protection is used. [CLIENT NAME] maintains site-specific fall protection plans for all Virginia projects.

## Hazard Communication (HazCom)

In compliance with OSHA 1926.59 (HazCom Standard):
- Safety Data Sheets (SDS) are maintained on-site for all hazardous chemicals
- All containers must be properly labeled
- Employees must receive training on hazardous chemicals they may be exposed to before beginning work

## Incident Reporting & Investigation

### Reporting Requirements
- **Fatality:** Report to OSHA within 8 hours (federal) or within 8 hours to VOSH for Virginia sites
- **Inpatient hospitalization, amputation, or loss of an eye:** Report to OSHA within 24 hours
- **All other injuries:** Report to supervisor within 24 hours; complete incident report form

### Investigation Process
1. Secure the scene and provide first aid as needed
2. Supervisor completes initial incident report within 24 hours
3. Investigation team (supervisor + safety officer) conducts root cause analysis within 72 hours
4. Corrective actions documented and implemented
5. Follow-up review at 30 days
